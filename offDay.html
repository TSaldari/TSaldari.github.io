Abdominals

Description: The abdominal muscles are a muscle group that is located on the front of the body on the abdomen. Its main function is to stabilize the trunk, and move the body between the ribcage and pelvis.

Muscle parts: The abdominals contains many muscles, these include the: Transversus abdominis, Rectus abdominis, External oblique muscles, and Internal oblique muscles.

Source: Physiopedia

Cardiovascular System

Description: The cardiovascular system is the transport system within the body for which blood is circulated. By circulating blood, oxygen is provided throughout the body, allowing for normal bodyily functions.

Benefits: Improving cardiovascular health can lead to a longer lifespan and enhanced performance in athletics.

System parts: The cardiovascular system is comprised of arteries, veins, capillaries, and the heart.

Source: Physiopedia
